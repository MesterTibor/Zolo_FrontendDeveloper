About the Danube River

The Danube is one of the most significant rivers in Europe, flowing through 10 countries and covering a length of around 2,850 kilometers. It originates in the Black Forest of Germany and flows southeast across Central and Eastern Europe before emptying into the Black Sea.

The river has played a crucial role in the cultural, economic, and historical development of the region, serving as a vital waterway for trade and transportation. Major cities like Vienna, Budapest, and Belgrade are located along its banks.
